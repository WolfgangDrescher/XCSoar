---
title: Bearing difference
description: Difference between the glider's track (direction of motion across the ground) and the bearing to the next waypoint, or for AAT tasks, the bearing to the target within the AAT sector. GPS navigation is based on the track, and the track may differ from the glider's heading when there is wind. Chevrons point to the direction the glider needs to alter course to correct the bearing difference, that is, to make it so that the glider is tracking directly toward the next waypoint. This calculation accounts for the curvature of the Earth.
